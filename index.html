Seaweed!

↓ ↓ ↓

Seaweeds resemble some common plant features such as roots, stems, and leaves, however are not closely related at all. The whole body of seaweed is called the thallus, the base which resembles roots is called the holdfast, the structure which resembles a stem is called stipe, and what look like leaves are referred to as blades, and in addition seaweeds have a flotation adaptation element called a pneumonia. There are 3 main seaweed groups; green algae, brown algae, red algae, and flowering plants which include; seagreasses, salt marsh plants, and mangroves.

Pneumatocysts are the gas filled bladder like structures of seaweeds, these are responsible for keeping the blades near the top of the water surface to maximize photosynthesis opportunities.

Some seaweeds have a stem-like structure for support called the stipe, this is where each blade originates from.

At the base of seaweeds, is the root-like structure called the holdfast, which attaches the whole thallus to the rocky hard bottom of the ocean floor. They are unable to attach to loose soft sediment bottoms for a rick of wave disruption.
